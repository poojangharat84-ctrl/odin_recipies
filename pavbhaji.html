Pav Bhaji

Pav Bhaji is a popular Indian street food dish that originated in Mumbai. It consists of a spicy and flavorful vegetable curry (bhaji) served with soft bread rolls (pav). The bhaji is made by cooking a variety of vegetables, such as potatoes, tomatoes, peas, and bell peppers, with a blend of spices. The pav is typically toasted with butter and served alongside the bhaji for dipping.

The bhaji is cooked until the vegetables are soft and well combined with the spices, creating a rich and flavorful curry. It is often garnished with chopped onions, fresh coriander leaves, and a squeeze of lemon juice for added freshness. The pav is toasted until it becomes slightly crispy on the outside while remaining soft on the inside.

Pav Bhaji is a beloved dish in Mumbai and has gained popularity across India and around the world. It is often enjoyed as a quick and satisfying meal or snack. The combination of the spicy bhaji and the buttery pav creates a delicious contrast of flavors that makes Pav Bhaji a favorite among food lovers.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (potatoes, tomatoes, peas, bell peppers)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)
  • Pav (soft bread rolls)

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  2. Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies to the pan and sauté for another minute until the raw smell disappears.
  3. Add the chopped vegetables to the pan and cook until they become soft and tender.
  4. Add the red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt to the pan. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes until the spices are well combined with the vegetables.
  5. Using a potato masher or the back of a spoon, mash the vegetables in the pan to create a thick and chunky curry. Add a little water if needed to adjust the consistency of the bhaji. Simmer for a few more minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  6. Once the bhaji is ready, garnish it with chopped coriander leaves and a squeeze of lemon juice. Serve the hot bhaji with toasted pav on the side. Enjoy!
