Misal Pav

Misal Pav is a popular Maharashtrian dish that consists of a spicy and flavorful curry made with sprouted moth beans (matki) and a variety of spices. It is typically served with pav, which are soft bread rolls. Misal Pav is known for its bold flavors and is often enjoyed as a breakfast or snack item.

The curry is made by cooking sprouted moth beans with a blend of spices, including red chili powder, turmeric, coriander powder, and garam masala. The curry is then garnished with chopped onions, fresh coriander leaves, and a squeeze of lemon juice for added freshness. The pav is usually toasted with butter and served alongside the curry, allowing you to dip the bread into the flavorful sauce. Misal Pav is often accompanied by a side of yogurt or curd to balance the spiciness of the curry.

Misal Pav is a beloved dish in Maharashtra and is enjoyed by people of all ages. It is a perfect combination of spicy, tangy, and savory flavors that make it a satisfying and delicious meal. Whether you are looking for a hearty breakfast or a flavorful snack, Misal Pav is sure to satisfy your taste buds.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup sprouted moth beans (matki)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)
  • Pav (soft bread rolls)

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  2. Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies to the pan and sauté for another minute until the raw smell disappears.
  3. Add the chopped tomatoes to the pan and cook until they become soft and mushy.
  4. Add the red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt to the pan. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes until the spices are well combined.
  5. Add the sprouted moth beans to the pan and mix well with the spice mixture. Add enough water to cover the beans and bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat and let it simmer until the beans are cooked and the curry thickens to your desired consistency.
  6. Once the curry is ready, garnish it with chopped coriander leaves and a squeeze of lemon juice. Serve the hot misal curry with toasted pav on the side. Enjoy!
