Dosa

Dosa is a popular South Indian dish that is made from fermented rice and urad dal (black gram) batter. It is a thin and crispy pancake that is typically served with chutney and sambar. Dosas are not only delicious but also healthy, as they are low in calories and rich in nutrients. The fermentation process enhances the nutritional value of the ingredients, making dosas a great choice for a wholesome meal.

To make dosas, the urad dal and rice are soaked separately, ground into a smooth batter, and then combined. The batter is left to ferment overnight, which gives the dosas their characteristic tangy flavor and crispy texture. Once fermented, the batter is poured onto a hot griddle or tawa and spread out in a circular motion to form a thin pancake. The dosa is cooked until it turns golden brown and crispy on the edges.

Dosas are not only a staple in South Indian cuisine but have also gained popularity across India and around the world due to their delicious taste and health benefits. They can be enjoyed as a breakfast or snack item and can be paired with a variety of chutneys, sambar, or even filled with savory fillings like potato masala for added flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 2 cups idli rice (parboiled rice)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed

Instructions

  1. Rinse the urad dal and idli rice separately under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the urad dal and fenugreek seeds (if using) in water for 4-6 hours or overnight. Soak the idli rice separately for the same duration.
  2. Drain the soaked urad dal and fenugreek seeds. Grind them together in a wet grinder or blender until you get a smooth and fluffy batter. Add water as needed to achieve the right consistency. The batter should be thick but pourable.
  3. Drain the soaked idli rice and grind it coarsely. You can add a little water to help with grinding, but be careful not to make it too watery.
  4. Combine the ground urad dal batter and the coarsely ground idli rice in a large bowl. Mix well to combine. The consistency of the batter should be similar to a thick pancake batter. Cover the bowl and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or overnight. The batter should rise and become airy.
  5. After fermentation, gently stir the batter. Add salt to taste and mix well. If the batter is too thick, you can add a little water to adjust the consistency.
  6. Heat a non-stick griddle or tawa over medium heat. Once hot, pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the griddle. Using the back of the ladle, spread the batter in a circular motion to form a thin pancake. Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa.
  7. Cook the dosa for 2-3 minutes, or until the edges start to turn golden brown and crispy. Flip the dosa and cook for another minute on the other side until it is cooked through and crispy. Remove the dosa from the griddle and repeat the process with the remaining batter.
