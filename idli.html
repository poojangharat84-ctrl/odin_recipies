Idli

Idli is a popular South Indian breakfast dish made from fermented rice and urad dal (black gram) batter. It is a soft and fluffy steamed cake that is typically served with chutney and sambar. Idlis are not only delicious but also healthy, as they are low in calories and rich in nutrients. The fermentation process enhances the nutritional value of the ingredients, making idlis a great choice for a wholesome breakfast.

To make idlis, the urad dal and rice are soaked separately, ground into a smooth batter, and then combined. The batter is left to ferment overnight, which gives the idlis their characteristic light and airy texture. Once fermented, the batter is poured into idli molds and steamed until cooked through. The result is a soft and fluffy idli that can be enjoyed with a variety of accompaniments.

Idlis are not only a staple in South Indian cuisine but have also gained popularity across India and around the world due to their delicious taste and health benefits. They are often enjoyed as a breakfast or snack item and can be paired with a variety of chutneys, sambar, or even a drizzle of ghee for added flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 2 cups idli rice (parboiled rice)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed

Instructions

  1. Rinse the urad dal and idli rice separately under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the urad dal and fenugreek seeds (if using) in water for 4-6 hours or overnight. Soak the idli rice separately for the same duration.
  2. Drain the soaked urad dal and fenugreek seeds. Grind them together in a wet grinder or blender until you get a smooth and fluffy batter. Add water as needed to achieve the right consistency. The batter should be thick but pourable.
  3. Drain the soaked idli rice and grind it coarsely. You can add a little water to help with grinding, but be careful not to make it too watery.
  4. Combine the ground urad dal batter and the coarsely ground idli rice in a large bowl. Mix well to combine. The consistency of the batter should be similar to a thick pancake batter. Cover the bowl and let the batter ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or overnight. The batter should rise and become airy.
  5. >After fermentation, gently stir the batter. Add salt to taste and mix well. If the batter is too thick, you can add a little water to adjust the consistency.
  6. Grease the idli molds with oil or ghee. Pour the batter into the molds, filling them about 3/4 full.
  7. Steam the idlis in a steamer or idli cooker for about 10-15 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of an idli comes out clean.
  8. Once cooked, remove the idlis from the molds and serve hot with chut ney and sambar.
